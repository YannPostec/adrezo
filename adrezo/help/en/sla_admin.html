Adrezo database feed

Data comes from Cacti, many scheduled jobs inject data in adrezo database :
  • daily, adrezo database is filled with Cacti device list evolutions (CactiDevicesJob).
    New devices are added, those disappeared are marked as destroyed (keep in database for statistic), those marked as disable in Cacti are marked disable as well in adrezo.
  • hourly, daily or monthly as defined in CactiStatsJob, Cacti availability statistics are injected in adrezo and resetted in Cacti database.
  • data are aggregated from hours to days, from days to month (CactiAggregateHoursJob, CactiAggregateDaysJob) to have monthly statistics

With proper permissions, you can manage differents items composing NAS.

Clients Management

Be careful if you delete a client, it will delete associated sites.

A client is defined by :

  • its name
  • visible or not in the statistics
  • associated planning (any change modify associated sites and devices)

Sites Management

Be careful, if you delete a site, all associated devices are put back in client/site Unknown.
Sites are a generic name for entity/zone/group of devices.

A site is defined by :

  • its client
  • its name
  • visible or not in the statistics
  • associated planning (any change modify associated devices)

Devices Management

Devices are imported into adrezo by scheduled jobs.
When created, they are in a special site "Unknown" for a special client "Unknown".
Thoses clients/sites doesn't appears in statistics.

A site is defined by many fields :

  • its site/client
  • its name
  • its Cacti ID for debugging purposes
  • its status, values are
    • 0 : Inactive (matches Disable in Cacti)
    • 1 : Active (matches Up/Enable in Cacti)
    • 2 : Destroyed (doesn't exist anymore in Cacti)
  • associated planning

Plannings Management

Be careful, if you delete a planning, all associated clients,sites,devices are put back in default planning (anytime).
Plannings define time periodes where statistics are not gathered.
