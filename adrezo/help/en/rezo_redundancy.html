Network Redundancy

With proper permissions, you can manage redundancy protocols.

Protocol Management

On this page, you will find a table with :
  • An Add zone on the first line
  • the list of existing protocols with their informations on the next lines
Within each line, you could :
  • Delete a protocol when you click on . Be careful, it will delete all associated redundancy IP.
  • Modify a protocol when you click on
When adding or modifying, icons appears.
Enter or modify informations then click on to validate.
You could also click on to cancel, going back to initial values.

A protocol is defined by only one field :

  • its name

Redundancy IP Addresse

On this page, you will find a table with :
  • An Add zone on the first line
  • the list of existing redundancy IP addresses with their informations on the next lines
Within each line, you could :
  • Delete a redundancy IP address when you click on
  • Modify a redundancy IP address when you click on
When adding or modifying, icons appears.
Enter or modify informations then click on to validate.
You could also click on to cancel, going back to initial values.

A redundancy IP address is defined by many fields :

  • its protocol
  • its ID
  • its IP address

IP address is choosed by clicking on , which opens a box allowing you to search by name or IP.
