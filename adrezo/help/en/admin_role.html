Role Management

With proper permissions,you can manage roles and associated access rights.

On this page, you will find a table with :
  • An Add zone on the first line
  • the list of existing roles with their informations on the next lines
Within each line, you could :
  • Delete a role when you click on
  • Modify a role when you click on
When adding or modifying, icons appears.
Enter or modify informations then click on to validate.
You could also click on to cancel, going back to initial values.
A role is defined by only one field :
  • its name
  • its directory
  • the initial context which be applied when user log into the application
  • rights applied for this role to a new context.
  • rights applied for this role for every existing context
  • if role is used with external authentication, the matching group used to choose this role
N.B. : Local directory roles as "NoAccess" and "AdminLocal" are not alterable.
N.B. : See users management to understand the notion of initial context.

New Context :

To define rights applied for this role in case of a new context, you could :
  • immediately fill the field with a value between 0 and 255
  • use the form by clicking on . This form presents all rights categories for this new context.

Rights Management :

Rights management is not available directly when creating a role. Once a role is created, you could modify rights by clicking on .
A form similar to the one with new context displaying one context per line.
Once you have done, validate by clicking on .
A reminder of rights and what they're used for is present in this form.

LDAP Groups Mapping :

Groups mapping is not available directly when creating a role. Once a role is created, you could choose those mappings.
After user authenticates, within authorize phase, the application look into every role of the directory the user logged into.
For each role, if there is a group mapping, the application checks if the user is a member of one of those groups.
If it is the case, role and rights are applied to the user, cumulatively with the previous ones.
To select a mapping click on wich display another window with groups listing.
